Welcome to DemonicSkull.com!

Ahoy there, swashbuckler! Welcome to DemonicSkull.com, the BEST fan site on the web dedicated to the Monkey Island series by LucasArts™ — and especially to the one and only Murray, the Demonic Skull!

Whether you've been sailing the seas of Mêlée Island since 1990, or you just picked up The Curse of Monkey Island last week, you've come to the right place. We've got game info, a complete insult sword fighting guide, and enough Murray quotes to keep you laughing for a fortnight!

Murray: I'm a powerful demonic force! I'm the harbinger of your doom! And the forces of darkness will applaud me as I STRIDE through the gates of hell carrying your head on a pike!

Guybrush: Stride?

Murray: All right then, ROLL! Roll through the gates of hell. Must you take the fun out of everything?

Murray Spotlight

Every week, we feature a classic Murray moment. This week's spotlight comes from the Carnival of the Damned in The Curse of Monkey Island:

Murray: I am Murray! The all-powerful demonic skull!

Guybrush: Murray, what are you doing here?

Murray: I have been placed here to torment you! To make your journey through this carnival of the damned a NIGHTMARE of EPIC proportions!

Guybrush: Really? They made you a ride operator?

Murray: ...Shut up.

Did You Know?

  • The name "Guybrush" came from the filename of his sprite in Deluxe Paint: "guy.brush" (the .brush extension was used for art files). "Threepwood" was borrowed from the P.G. Wodehouse novels!
  • The SCUMM engine stands for "Script Creation Utility for Maniac Mansion" and was used in all LucasArts adventure games!
  • Murray first appeared in The Curse of Monkey Island (1997) and quickly became one of the most beloved characters in the series.
  • You can't actually die in LucasArts adventure games! It was a design philosophy — unlike those Sierra games where you die every 5 minutes. ;-)
  • Ron Gilbert has said that the Secret of Monkey Island was partly inspired by the Pirates of the Caribbean ride at Disneyland and Tim Powers' novel On Stranger Tides.
